The problem

Most advertisers never unlock the AI already inside their ad platforms.

Every ad platform runs on machine learning, and that machine learning runs on the conversion data you feed it. Most “optimization” stops at creative tweaks, keyword edits, and bid adjustments: indirect moves inside the dashboard. The most fundamental lever, improving the quality of the training data itself, goes untouched. Three problems get left on the table:

Not enough data

  • Signal loss from cookie deprecation and ATT
  • Mismatches from missing parameters
  • Too few conversions for the algorithm to learn from

Low-quality data

  • Every conversion sent with identical value
  • No signal to tell a great customer from a one-time buyer
  • Deep conversion points never reach the platform

Real outcomes never make it back

  • Platform data drifts from actual business results
  • Learning stays a black box
  • No mechanism to correct what the algorithm got wrong

How it works

A self-learning flywheel that keeps improving your ad performance.

01

Track: deeper than pixels can go.

Cross-app and cross-domain journeys are usually where measurement dies. Server-side tracking identifies and follows users even as they move between the web and other apps, so no conversion path goes dark.

↓
02

Structure: turn everything into signal.

Chat transcripts, on-site behavior, form inputs, first-party and zero-party data. These messy, unstructured sources are automatically processed and converted into usable, structured signals.

↓
03

Score: predict the revenue behind each conversion.

The prediction model at the core of the engine estimates pLTV, the future expected revenue of each conversion, and scores every conversion in a form ad platforms can actually use.

↓
04

Sync: tailor the data to each platform's algorithm.

Predicted pLTV scores are enriched and formatted into the exact signal shape each platform's AI learns from best, maximizing user match rates inside Meta, Google, and beyond.

↓
05

Verify: close the loop with real outcomes.

Actual downstream results flow back to the platform automatically, so predicted scores get checked against reality. The platform learns from the difference, correcting its own dataset with every cycle.

↓
06

Compound: collective intelligence raises accuracy for everyone.

Anonymized, statistically processed conversion data feeds the shared prediction model, so accuracy keeps climbing. The model gets better with every account it learns from.

Case studies

Working across industries where the real conversion happens late.

Education

Survey answers predicted enrollment: ROAS 180% in two months.

This advertiser measured success at chat-app registration. We added a short survey and used the answers to weight each conversion by its likelihood of becoming a paid enrollment, sending those weighted signals to the platform. ROAS rose week over week for two months as delivery concentrated on high-intent users only.

Healthcare staffing

Moving the learning point cut interview CPA by 41%.

The original conversion point was a landing-page link tap. We switched platform learning to survey respondents who matched the real hiring profile: licensed, aged 20–40, actively switching jobs within six months. Interview CPA dropped 41% within one month.

Luxury real estate

Found the cluster that books meetings: meeting CPA down 33%.

Offline conversions from the CRM weren't producing sales meetings. From form fields such as income, demographics, and location, we identified the feature cluster that actually converts to meetings and sent that signal to the platform, cutting meeting CPA by a third.

Common questions

What advertisers ask us first.

Which industries does this work for?

Beauty salons, spas, aesthetic clinics, real estate, staffing and recruiting agencies, SaaS, and education: businesses where the real conversion happens off-platform or long after the click see the biggest gains, because that's where default platform learning is weakest.

Our bookings run on Square and we can't add pixels. Does this still work?

Yes, that's exactly the case we built for. When your booking platform doesn't allow custom pixels or GTM, we optimize your booking flow to capture the signals the platform can't, then send qualified conversions and lifetime value back to the ad platform server-side. Your ads learn from real customers instead of link clicks.

What does "curated conversions" actually mean?

Only genuinely valuable actions, such as form submissions, booked visits, and closed deals, get sent to the ad platform as conversions. Training the platform exclusively on high-LTV users steers its auto-optimization in the direction you actually want.

Can we change the conversion point later?

Yes. A common pattern is starting with a shallower conversion to accelerate learning, then switching to a deeper one as data accumulates: a staged tuning approach you can iterate on a daily cadence.

What can it connect to?

The CAPIs of Meta, Google, Yahoo, and LINE Ads, plus HubSpot, Pardot, Klaviyo, KARTE, Tealium, Treasure Data, and chat-based CRMs. Some integrations are in beta, so ask us about your stack.

We don't have engineers in-house. Is that a problem?

Not at all. A dedicated lead handles discovery, conversion-point design, and implementation end to end. Because the integration is adapted to your existing booking flow and tools, expect setup to take about a month. We handle the technical work throughout.

Can it handle multiple accounts or campaigns at once?

Yes. You can design unlimited conversion points per initiative, and data can be unified across accounts.
